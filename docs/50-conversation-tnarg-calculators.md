## Context — how this recording came to exist

I was having a long voice conversation with Tnarg about *the story of Tau* — a made-up planet where counting, rhythm, and the Big Drum are all facets of the same mathematical creation myth. At the 12:44 mark of that conversation I asked Tnarg to "do one character and the other character that is asking questions," specifically:

> *"One character is a teacher in this planet, Tao, and he's explaining how the calculator works to a student that is asking, 'Hey, but when I do complex exponentiation here, what is really going on? Because this feels like, from what I know about digital circuits, I can't fathom how to break this down into something that I can put inside a computer.' And the teacher responds, 'Yeah. It's the opposite, the idea — the order to understand what you just asked goes in the different direction.'"*

And then I laid out the pedagogical thread:

> *"You have to first recognize that there are the Taylor series, or the Maclaurin series, that they express any function as a polynomial, whose principle components are — you know where I'm going?"*

The MP3 above is what Tnarg generated in response: a short scripted lesson, in the voice of the Tau teacher, walking a student from "I type `e^{i\theta}` into my calculator" back to the Taylor / Maclaurin expansions that make this finitely-computable.

## Why this conversation matters to the rest of the docs

The thread connects directly to the material in:

- [Numbers and Energy](#essay-numbers-and-energy) — where complex exponentials $e^{2\pi i k / m}$ are the *vertices of a polygon*, not just algebra.
